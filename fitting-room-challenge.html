Background

When picturing a jogger, It is quite obvious people have a specific mental image in mind: Fit, sweaty, wearing running bottoms and a tank top."JEANS" is definitely on the list of "what NOT to wear for sport."

However, Earl Jean Taiwan launched a new product called "Cool knitted Denim", the jeans you can sport for sports, which are easy to move in, breathable and moisture-wicking.

Client

Earl Jean Taiwan

Challenge

- How to introduce the product when jeans are the last type of pants that people would wear for sports?
- How to make the distinction when every denim brand emphasize the same product features?

Insight

Trying on jeans before buying is the most important rule to find one pair that fits. To see is to believe. Time people spent in the fitting room is almost a third of the in-store time. It's also the best and right moment to let the product speak for itself. The fitting room can be a total sales killer!

Idea

Fitting Room Challenge

- First Step: Experiential Marketing
We transformed Earl Jean's fitting room into a real life Olympic challenge. As soon as the unsuspecting shoppers entered the fitting room and put the jeans on, the floor began to move like a treadmill, forcing them to run as quick as they can to keep balance. Then they could discover the flexibility and well-fitting of "Cool knitted Denim".

- Second Step: A Viral Video
We put these funny reactions of shoppers into a video and released it on Earl Jean FB and Youtube channel. Through the video, The Earl Jean successfully aligned itself a sense of fun and energy, communicated its product features to its target audience "It is the jeans that people could wear for sports".

01

Installation

We created a normal-looking fitting room, with a treadmill in it. When customers experienced the jeans in speed running, their performance were all recorded by the hidden cameras inside the fitting rooms.

O2O Commerce

02

After publishing online viral videos, we set this interactive fitting room device in a particular retail store, drawing potential customers from online channels to physical stores. In addition, in order to invite people to experience the jeans on the treadmill, we also set a location-based advertising to send discount codes to people when they passed by the store.
